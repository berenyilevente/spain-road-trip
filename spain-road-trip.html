Cartagena

Roman port city

A beautifully preserved 2,000-year-old Roman theatre sits in the middle of a lively naval harbour town. Easy half-day stop. Theatre closed Mondays.

The wild card and the highlight for beaches — a desert national park with the most undeveloped coves in Spain. No facilities at the best ones, so bring shade and water.

The one inland detour

Worth leaving the coast for. The Alhambra is the single best thing on the whole trip. Book Nasrid Palace tickets weeks ahead — timed entry is mandatory and sells out.

Quick reference

◍ Beaches

  • Playa de Mónsul — Cabo de GataThe famous golden cove. Wild, no facilities.
  • Los Genoveses — Cabo de GataLong, calm, undeveloped bay.
  • Playa Burriana — NerjaBest beach on the coast stretch, with beach bars.
  • Playa del Postiguet — AlicanteEasy city beach to start the trip.

◍ Party spots

  • Puerto Banús / MarbellaThe glitzy one — marina & beach clubs.
  • El Barrio — AlicanteOld-town streets, wall-to-wall bars.
  • Soho & the port — MálagaGood scene for the final night.
  • Benidorm — 45 min off-routeThe coast's party capital, if you want one blowout.

◍ Must-visit

  • Alhambra — GranadaThe standout. Book Nasrid Palace tickets early.
  • Roman Theatre — Cartagena2,000 years old. Closed Mondays.
  • Frigiliana — above NerjaThe prettiest white hill village.
  • Old town & Alcazaba — MálagaMoorish fortress + Picasso, a great finish.

◍ Need to know

  • One-way rentalPick up Alicante, drop Málaga — different regions, so expect a drop-off fee.
  • July is peakRental prices run well above average — book early.
  • Alhambra ticketsTimed entry, sells out. Lock these in first.
  • Driving is light~6h total over 7 days — lots of slack to linger.
03

The car

Targeting €200–300 total

Doable, but tight. July is the most expensive month to rent in Alicante (economy ~€25–30/day), and the one-way drop fee adds roughly €100–150. Realistic total for 6 days lands around €250–330.

To stay near the bottom: book now, take an economy/compact manual, and choose the all-inclusive / no-excess rate to avoid big deposit holds. Compare on Rentalcars.com or AutoEurope; Goldcar, Malco, Wiber and Enterprise all run the Alicante→Málaga route.

Tip: if a cheap return-to-Alicante deal exists, a loop can sometimes beat the one-way fee — worth a quick price check.
